In this task you create pictures by selecting and/or deselecting symbols presented in a row across {{ num_pictures }} columns (see screenshot below). There are 10 different symbols and each row of symbols represents a picture. An alien from outer space likes to buy such pictures and it will pay different amounts for different pictures with different symbols, but you don’t know what the alien likes and how the interconnectedness between the different symbols influences the payoff. A first example picture and its payoff are already displayed.

You create every picture jointly with another participant who you are randomly allocated to. One of you is P1, the other one is P2. Both of you are allocated to one area with five symbols that you can select or deselect. P1 is responsible for the five symbols on the left, P2 is responsible for the five symbols on the right, and both of you can freely change any symbol in your area, but you cannot make changes to symbols in the other participant’s area. In every round, you can change as many symbols as you wish (0-5), but you must make a decision for every symbol in every round as the alien considers for the evaluation of the picture the entire combination from both areas.

Before you change any symbols, you must chat with the other participant. When both of you decide to end the chat, and click on the corresponding button, you and the other participant will be able to change the symbols in your area. When you’re ready with your choice of selected and not-selected buttons in your area, you can click on the “Submit” button. After your and the other participants choices are submitted, the ‘P1 Payoff’ and the ‘P2 Payoff’ will then be shown in addition to the ‘Joint Payoff’, which is the accumulation of both individual payoffs. With the display of these payoffs, the round is completed, and you can continue with the chat for the next round.

While you are not able to return to prior rounds, the alien is willing to buy the same combination more than once; the payoff will then stay the same. Also note that you and the other participant have the exact same information, i.e. you see all selected and not-selected symbols and payoffs in both areas in all prior rounds.

The alien buys all the pictures that you jointly create in 4 rounds. The alien pays you the accumulated ‘Joint Payoff’; the other participant also receives this payoff. For this task, you will earn a bonus of 2 pence (£0.02) per 10 points.

In this task you create pictures by selecting and/or deselecting symbols presented in a row across 12 columns (see screenshot below). There are 12 different symbols, and each row of symbols represents a picture. An alien from outer space likes to buy such pictures and it will pay different amounts for different pictures with different symbols, but you don’t know what the alien likes and how the interconnectedness between the different symbols influences the payoff. A first example picture and its payoff are already displayed.

On each trial you can combine symbols any way you like to make pictures: you can select or deselect as many symbols as you wish (0-10), but you must make a decision for every symbol in every trial as the alien considers the entire combination of selected and not-selected symbols. When you're ready you can click on the "Submit" button and the price will then be shown under the heading "Payoff" and the trial is completed.

After submitting 12 pictures, the alien buys all pictures you created and pays you the accumulated price. The value of the pictures will change when you move to the next alien who will purchase the next block of 12 pictures.

While you are not able to return to prior trials or blocks, all aliens are willing to buy the same combination more than once; the payoff will then stay the same.

The aliens buy all the pictures that you create and pay you the accumulated ‘Total Payoff’. For this task, you will earn a bonus of 2 pence (£0.02) per 10 points.
